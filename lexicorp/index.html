FINAL TALLY
    HOW TO PLAY

    Goal: a game for 2–5 players — build words to earn money and stock, patent the letters you use to collect royalties, and finish with the most wealth.

    On your turn, do one of two things:

    • Play a word in two steps. Step 1: build a word from your 7 hand cards plus the 3 shared pool cards — 3+ letters, using at least one card from your hand. Step 2: review what you (and any patent owners) will earn, optionally buy a patent, then end your turn.
    • Discard & redraw — discard any number of hand cards, draw that many back, and end your turn (no score). Or use Swap whole hand if you're stuck.

    Tips: tap any patent chip to see its cost, owner and power (powered letters are marked ★). Drag your hand letters to rearrange them at any time — even when it isn't your turn.

    Earn: longer words pay more money, and 6+ letter words also pay stock. A word containing a Q pays double money and stock. Stock can't be spent — it's worth points at the end.

    Patent a letter you played from a card (one per turn) by paying its cost to the bank. After that, each time an opponent plays a card with that letter you collect $1 from the bank. You never earn royalties on your own patents, and letters added by a power (Z's S, X's reused card) pay none.

    Seven patents carry powers — B J K Q V X Z: double a vowel-bookended / vowel-heavy / single-vowel word, play a second word, reuse a card, add a free S, or swap a card at the start of your turn. A Y in your word can be set as a vowel or consonant, which matters for those doublers.

    Game end: once a player's total patent value reaches the goal for the table size (2p $45 · 3p $34 · 4p $26 · 5p $21), the round is completed so everyone has had equal turns, then play stops. Most money + stock + patent value wins (ties go to patent value).
