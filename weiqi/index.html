How to play

The idea

Weiqi (Go) is a game of surrounding territory. Black and White take turns placing a stone on any empty crossing. Black plays first. You never move a stone once it's down — you only lose stones by being captured. When both players pass, whoever controls more of the board wins.

New to the game?

Start with Training from the New Game menu — 15 short, guided lessons take you from placing your first stone to capturing techniques like the ladder and double atari.

Placing a stone

On your turn, tap an empty crossing to preview your stone (it shows as a shadow, with any captures noted). Tap the same spot again, or press Play, to confirm. Tap a different point to move the preview.

Capturing & liberties

A stone or connected group is captured when its last liberty (adjacent empty point) is filled. Captured stones are lifted off and counted. You may not play a stone with no liberties (suicide) unless the move captures. A stone can't immediately retake a single stone in a repeating position (the ko rule).

Board sizes

Beginner is 9×9 (fast), Intermediate 13×13, and Full the classic 19×19. The host picks the size when creating the game.

Ending & scoring

When there's nothing useful left, pass. Two passes in a row end the game. Your score is your stones on the board plus the empty points you've surrounded; White also gets 6.5 komi for going second. Capture or surround dead stones before you pass — the count assumes the position is settled.

Turn notifications

Tap the 🔔 in the menu to be notified when it becomes your turn while the game is in the background. With Web Push set up, notifications arrive even if the browser is fully closed. On iPhone, first add Weiqi to your Home Screen (Share → Add to Home Screen) and open it from there.

Offline, leaving & resigning

Weiqi is happily asynchronous — an opponent going offline is fine; play resumes when they return. Leave just exits to your games list. Resign gives up the game for good: your opponent wins and it's removed from your list.
